**[Profile/About]{.underline}**

I am Court Granville, a multidisciplinary designer guided by a
deep-seated curiosity about how the world and the things/systems within
it work. Trained primarily as a product designer, I have leaned towards
that throughout my studies. But my extracurricular interest in
technology, AI-enabled programming and systems has pushed me in a
different direction as I embark on my career. What drives me is solving
problems for people in thoughtful, meaningful and beautiful ways, a
principle that I have sought with all of my work, to varying degrees of
success as I navigate learning about the world of design and how it can
be used to positively impact the systems, industries and products it
touches. I have also become mostly interested in working in technical
industries, often those dominated by scientists, engineers, and
developers. My belief is that design is naturally positioned to be
essential in translating the technical into something that can be
understood, enjoyed, bought and used effectively by people around the
world.

AI has helped me foster this interest into something tangible, as it has
greatly lowered the barrier of entry for designers interested in working
with technical subjects. My most recent example of this is my
Undergraduate Thesis, which, through AI, morphed from a series of
physical posters into an interactive, animated, tangible, and
emotionally provocative design intervention accessible to anyone in the
world via the web. This would not have been possible without AI, and so,
while I am distinctly aware of its limitations, I cannot help but see it
as a huge multiplier for designers who seek to use it properly, not just
to "do their homework". I hope my portfolio of work demonstrates my
curiosity, my willingness to dive into things headfirst and work my way
through problems, and my ability to adapt and overcome different briefs
with a variety of different constraints and approaches.

I am not someone who shies away from the academic, the technical or the
analytical. My background in History, Art History and Maths is not a
typical entry point into design, but it has given me a unique
perspective on how design fits into the world as a "problem solver", and
occasionally as a problem creator. I have a passion for work that
genuinely improves people's lives and not just adds more to them. My
belief is that truly beautiful design is the kind that sparks curiosity,
fulfilment and long-lasting satisfaction in its users. This interest in
both technical subjects and improving people's lives has naturally led
me to work surrounding complex contested technologies -- the energy
industry specifically -- and I hope to continue this trend in my future
work.

**WABI SABI \| Product Design \| 2022**

- The WABI SABI project was an ambitious product design project that
  introduced our class to 3D modelling. The brief was to create a
  project that could be sold by the company MUJI, known for its
  minimalist "this will do" approach to design that has made it wildly
  popular globally.

- Our project was named Wabi Sabi after the Japanese principle of
  "Beauty in serenity, simplicity and imperfection".

- My group decided to experiment with 3D printing to develop a universal
  phone case for Apple iPhones. We would have wanted to design a
  universal phone case for all phones, but given the scope and budget
  constraints of our project, we decided to limit ourselves to iPhones
  ranging from the iPhone SE to the iPhone 14 (the most recent at the
  time of the project).

- Our case utilised a flexible 3D filament so it could expand and
  contract to fit the iPhone\'s size. Through many stages of iteration,
  we managed to get a final prototype that was functional and worked for
  all the phones in our range.

- We kept the design very minimal, in line with the MUJI brief, with the
  only additional feature being padded corners to better protect the
  phones when using the case. The flexible filament also served as a
  relatively good protection mechanism due to it being far more
  forgiving than typical brittle 3D filament.

**GRAVITY WAVE BACKGAMMON BOARD \| Product Design \| 2024**

- The Gravity Wave project followed a brief provided by the company that
  makes the recycled plastic material. Our instructions were to take a
  1m x 1m board of this material and increase its value in some way.

- My group decided to make a backgammon board for two reasons: the
  material\'s visual characteristics and the high value and
  craftsmanship of backgammon boards.

- The initial prototypes were 3D-modelled, CNC'd from wood, and
  assembled to iterate on our design before using our 1m x 1m board.

- Due to limitations in the brief that didn't allow us to use any other
  materials for the final product, we had to find a way to distinguish
  between the "black" and "white" pieces/markers on the board. We
  noticed that if you CNC'd the material down to between 1-3mm, varying
  degrees of light were able to make it through the material. Through
  this, we added indented sections to our 3D model for the board layout,
  alternating layer thicknesses so that we could use lights to create
  the typical backgammon board pattern on the material\'s surface,
  whilst keeping the surface entirely flat and smooth.

- The WAVE Speaker was a development in my interest in electronics and
  product design.

- It was the culmination of a group project in which the brief was to
  deliver 10 different deliverables every week for 10 weeks. My group's
  primary interests were electronics, woodworking, audio design, and
  product design.

- We spent many weeks working with different wood samples, CNC
  machining, 3D printing internal components, and researching sound and
  speaker design.

- The final product took a broken pair of Edifier speakers, fixed them

  case. The case itself was CNC'd, sanded and finished, while the
  internal components were 3D printed and CNC'd.

**The Nuclear Question \| Data Visualisation / Information Design /
Interaction Design / Web Design / UX+UI \| 2026**

- Built <https://thenuclearquestion.com> as a public-facing platform,
  including interactive data visualisation tools that make nuclear
  strengths, weaknesses and risk data navigable for non-technical
  audiences.

- Examines the architecture of public communication around nuclear
  energy and, by extension, contested decarbonisation technologies - how
  ownership, financing and risk framings move from technical literature
  into mainstream discourse (UK focus).

- Conducted expert interviews (nuclear-sector investor; senior PR
  practitioner) and an original public-perception survey (n=60) on
  misconceptions of nuclear risk, cost, and waste

  IPCC AR5, UNECE 2021 lifecycle estimates, Ember UK generation data,
  and peer-reviewed energy-mortality figures (Markandya & Wilkinson,
  2007; Sovacool et al., 2016)

- Frames the designer as an \"epistemic facilitator\" - aligned with
  investigative data journalism and climate-accountability traditions.

**[Portfolio Design]{.underline}**

I want my portfolio to stand out without trying to do too much that
distracts viewers from my work, who I am, and what I stand for. Emphasis
must be placed on both the quality of the layout/design of the
portfolio, with the primary function of my portfolio being to show my
work, and it must not be difficult for the viewer to navigate their way
to my projects and work, as well as the quality of the interaction
between the viewer and the website itself. Crucially, I don't want to
use generic portfolio designs; I want my portfolio to be truly unique
and representative of my interests as described above in my profile.

I want the website to demonstrate my skills as an AI-assisted web
developer (with a level of interaction similar to my thesis website,
thenuclearquestion.com), and to be beautifully designed from a UX/UI
perspective, comparable to the portfolios shown on the website
Awwwards.com. The design system I want to use must be refined, carefully
selected, and beautiful. It must not be a typical "Claude" design system
with a beige background and random CTAs that don't point to anything. It
must be a design system worthy of a professional designer, not just some
random person wanting to create a website.

The workflow for the creation of the portfolio will be to use Claude
Desktop to create a plan, explore different design systems and research
examples of portfolios, as well as web development tools, that I could
take inspiration from/use (especially on the interaction/animation/UX/UI
fronts -- this could also include researching designers who have similar
interests to me and seeing how they approached their portfolios) \>
Claude Design to build the initial framework and outlines of the website
\> extract the files to local computer and upload to github for version
management \> use Claude Code to iterate the design and layout, as well
as being able to access local files such as images of my projects so
that my portfolio can host high definition images of my work \>
cloudflare pages to host my site on courtgranville.com.\
\
It is absolutely critical that we take the first stages slowly,
especially the research phases, where we set out a plan for how to get
the most "me" website possible. I don't want to shy away from the
technical side, so I need ample time to prepare resources and not go in
blind.
