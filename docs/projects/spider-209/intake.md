## What this project carries

From the plan (§2): **fabrication fidelity, and the confidence to learn by replicating a master.** The page should leave a reader thinking: *this person chose to reproduce a Joe Colombo design faithfully, and understands why that is a sophisticated thing to do — not a shortcut.*

## What's on disk (from the audit)

2 sketch images + `sketch-details.pdf`, 4 prototype shots, 3 studio shots, `Brief.pdf`. No CAD — this was hand-drawn and hand-made, and that fact is itself part of the story.

## Key decisions  *(you — the heart of the page)*

## Making & iteration  *(you)*

## Resolved  *(you)*

## Reflection  *(you — the most important answer)*

**10.** What did reproducing a master's design teach you — about Colombo's decisions, about your own making, about design itself? This is the line that turns "a student copied a lamp" into "a designer learned to think like Colombo by rebuilding his lamp."
